Evolution of Automobiles

Cugnot's Fardier a vapeur

1770

First self-propelled vehicle

The first ever car-like machine was built in 1770 by Nicolas-Joseph Cugnot who built a self-propelled vehicle, well we can't really call this a car, because it has no engine. This machine had several problems tho, the amount of water this could have wasn't much and the steam pressure was not strong enough to get this trycycle going faster. It was going at a speed of 3.6 kilometers per hour (3.6km/h) when carrying 4 passengers which was the max capacity it could carry, but the top speed was 7.8 km/h when there was only the driver. Overall this vehicle had 3 wheels, one front, 2 wheels back, supported a steam boiler and the driving mechanism did exist. In 2010 a replica of this machine was made and worked perfectly well.

Puffing Devil

1801

First steam-powered passenger vehicle

Some 30 years later after Cugnot's Fardier a vapeur, Richard Trevithick who is born in Cornwall, England, built a locomotive-like vehicle that was named "Puffing Devil". The steam engine was made by Scotsman James Watt, which had a pressure of 145 psi (pounds per square inch). It had a pressure-operated piston connected to a cylindrical horizontal boiler, but litteraly few days later the machine was destroyed due to overheat and burned down. It originally rode on rails, but there was the second problem that it was so heavy, it broke it's rails and retired after 3 trips. In 2017 was made the replica.

Hippomobile

1863

First gas engine powered vehicle

The first gas engine was made by Jean Joseph Etienne Lenoir who was a Belgian engineer. The international combustion engine was made using a jumping spark ignition system where sparks ignited a coal gas mixture and oxygen inside a one cylinder engine. This vehicle got into production and by 1865, 143 of these were sold in Paris and the production of the engine in London begun. After Joseph's death, a month had passed and on July 16, 1900, the Automobile Club of France said - "In recognition of his great merits as an inventor of the gas engine and builder of the first car in the world". This message didn't age well because it was never meant to be a car, but an engine. Originally it was this motor that became popular and later he just slapped the engine on a 3-wheeled wagon, but it still remains a vehicle that is powered with a 1-cylinder engine and has a little car looking that with a headlight.

Grenville Steam Carriage

1875

Much faster vehicle in the 1880s powered by a steam engine

Its a driveable vehicle that was built around a boiler from a Merryweather & Sons fire engine, that could carry total of 8 people. A collabration began in 1874, a team planned to make steam-powered road vehicle that can carry passengers, and the collabration lasted for 15 years. It has a steering wheel which is turned by a tiller at the front, and 2 wheels on the rear, lying on the driven axle. There are 2 parallel seats that can carry 6 passengers. You actually need several people to keep this thing going, a fireman needs to sit at the rear, stock the fire and preserve the water level in the boiler. The brake does exist which is the foot pedal and this thing can go 32 km/h, but consuming a ton of supplies, (about 20 liters of water and 2.5 kg of coal per mile).

1875

First car concept

A person named Siegried Marcus built a automobile in 1875 which runned on gasoline and powered by a 4-cycle engine. We can still call this thing a car because it was a driveable gasoline powered vehicle. Its known as the first vehicle that used gasoline as fuel. He made several different models and the 3rd and 4th version did not survive on the road, this is why we call this the second car, it was the only vehicle that had survived on the road and was actually driveable. Originally he began building the self-propelled car in 1860, then 15 years later the second car concept was finished in his factory in Vienna. Firstly equipped with a 2-cycle engine and later a 4-cycle one. The controversy is that this machine is known as the first car ever made, and thats not true, because it wasn't a production vehicle, so its not replacing the Benz Patent-Motorwagen first place. The wheels were made out of steel where Benz came and put rubber tires to the wheels, because it wasn't meant to be a train, but a car. Now we don't call this 100kg engine "Pressure", but now its horsepower, this thing had 0.9 hp at 400 rpm (rpm is car acceleration), and was capable of going 16 km/h (10 mph). Later he produced more powerful models, one of them were named Stahlradwagen which was the nr 2 model that had a 1.5hp engine and model nr 3 having 2hp engine. The main materials were steel and wood.

Firstly coming to the job, he didn't have much education, but on one day at Lohner-Werke, he built a drivetrain with low-friction by mounting the motors to the hubs. The motors had about 3 - 7hp. Its kind of a long story tho, so I'm not going to talk about it here, but the vehicle was finished in 1900, which required about 2 tons of 80 volt and lead-acid batteries that had to be encased in a spring-suspended container to protect the cells. The batteries had a power of 1,280 electric motors that moved this 4.4 ton of a vehicle. Overall this thing turned out be a litteral tank with 56 hp, and Porsche was ahead of his time.
