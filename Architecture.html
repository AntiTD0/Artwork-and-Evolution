Evolution of architecture

Prehistoric Times (11,600 - 3,500 BCE)

These kind of styles were built in the Stone Age, Iron Age and Bronze Age. Most common materials in building were mud, stone and wood, sometimes were just several huge stones to make a shelter. Pretty much prehistoric styles were the minimalism in architecture, even decorations didn't exist. Humans often created megaliths, stone circles and huts. The most mysterious designs are the circle-shaped ones - It has been guessed by archaeologists that the prehistoric people looked to the heavens to replicate the sun and moon. Overall people primarily made them for sheds.

Ancient Egypt (3,050 - 900 BCE)

In Ancient Egypt architecture, the most known ones are the pyramids. In Egyptian it was called "myr" and due to their shape Egyptians created obelisks on top of the pyramids which is called primordial mound, where they tought that the earth was created this way, including in night time, the dark areas with stars meant to be a physical gateway into the heavens, where the Great Pyramid points towards directly to the center of this part of the sky. There are actually more styles, even containing little towns, palaces, tombs, fortresses and temples. They were made out of locally avaible limestone and mud bricks, but sometimes sandstone and granite. Often in building there were alot of people helping, decorating the walls with hundreds of hieroglyphs and carvings, painted with yellow and brown colors. Pretty much humans were like slaves building these monuments and today these buildings have almost vanished due to sunlight and being near the river Nile, where it have risen a ton over thousands of years, but some of them have survived too, being on high ground and made out of stone which is sufficiently strong not to break in the millenniums.

Classical (850 BCE - CE 476)

Its received from the ancient Rome-and Greece architecture. There have been alot of different styles of classical architecture existing since the Carolingian-and Italian Renaissance. This style have often used many poles (also called columns), which are sometimes extremely decorated - they are called orders and there are 5 types of them, belonging to ancient Greece. The first one is the Grecian Doric order - Its the most simple one, easily decorated with circular capitals at the top of columns. The second one is the Ionic order - These are pretty detailed ones, used a right angle, straight-edge, a compass and lastly a string to establish half-lenghts. The third one is the Corinthian order - Its the most complex one, combination of Ionic, noted for it's developed capitals decorated with acanthus leaves and scrolls. The forth one is the Tuscan order, its the simpliest one and lastly the Composite order which has the most royal looking. For summary the orders are like decoration upgrades and also vocubulary rules, from Tuscan to Composite.

Byzantine (527 - 565)

Byzantine architecture is the architecture of the Eastern Roman Empire or Byzantine Empire. When the Roman Empire became Christian, which was extended to east, with its new capital at Constantinople, the architecture became ambitious. The byzantine design is known for it's more exotic, richer in mosaics and large open spaces at the centers of churches. People decided to use bricks and stones as main material, windows as these vivid wonderful golden mosaics to bring light and warmth into the heart of churches, domed roofs and classical forms. Some of the interiors and columns were made out of richer material which was marble.

Romenesque (800 - 1200)

This medieval Europe architecture can occassionally look like a castle, ranging from tiny chapels to cathedrals. In these times there were Carolingian, Merovingian and Ottonian architects who kept building large stone monasteries, churches, palaces with small windows and thick walls. These large buildings primary idea were meant to intimidate and inspire. Some of these buildings used sculpture to illstrate church doctrine and Biblical scenes. For summary the main idea for the Romanesque architecture was to build a statement of wealth and power.

Gothic (1100 - 1450)

Its an architecture style that has envolved from the Romanesque architecture and succeeded Renaissance architecture. These cathedrals and other huge buildings rose to the new heights, the style is litteraly Romanesque but higher ceilings. Gothic style (also known as French style) is originated in the lle-de-France and Picardy regions of northern France, and later became extremely popular due to it's appearance, which is basically a combination of Romanesque architecture. This architecture has never died honestly, because it was reawakened in the 19th and 20th centuries where architects in the United States and Europe designed houses imitating the cathedral architecture which we today also call Gothic Revival.

Renaissance (1400-1600)

Renaissance is the rebirth of Classical culture that is originated in Florence in the early 15th century which quickly spread to Europe, countries like Germany, France, Spain, England, Russia and other states in Europe. This style followed Gothic architecture and was successful with it's Baroque style. The main key was the classical antiquity, proportion, geometry and symmetry. Renaissance architecture have 3 main periods which are Early Renaissance, High Renaissance and Late Renaissance which is also called Mannerism. High Renaissance was the time when these buildings were everywhere beautifully flourishing. There are made several paintings that represent Renaissance architecture and know are one of the most popular ones in the world.

Baroque (1600-1830)

Baroque architecture have used many decorations with complex shapes and bold contrasts. The High Baroque was in 1625-1675 when it was used in palaces and churches, including Portugal, France, Spain, Austria and Bavaria, in the late Baroque period which was in 1675-1750, reached all the way to Russia. Baroque architects took several common elements of Renaissance architecture where are domes and colonnades, basically made them taller, more decorated, dramatic and irreverent. Commonly the ceilings had these dramatic light effects made by quadratura. Overall this design flourished in the 17-18th century in the central Europe and was one of the larger movements in art and design.

Rococo (1650-1790)

Rococo is known as late baroque, another dramatic style of architecture which uses pastel colors, scrolling curves and asymmetry. It began in the 1730s, France and spread to northern Italy, Austria and more other Europe states. Rococo has influenced arts like music, painting, furniture and sculpture. Sometimes the insides of Rococo-styled buildings acted like theatres where there are showed people like they are moving. There are often represented still life, including, plants, flowers, musical instruments and fruits. The interiors have also used sculptures which were made out of cement, including dragons, angels, monkeys, Chinese people and weird-looking flowers.

Neoclassicism (1730-1925)

Neoclassical architecture has blank walls, Roman detail which is Doric and simple geometric forms. There are several designs in Neoclassical architecture, including domed roofs and flat roofs. It began to spread in Europe in the 1750s, and flourished in the 18th, 19th centuries. USA decided to use ancient greek style on the government buildings to make them look like the birthplace of democracy. These classical architectures like Romanesque, Renaissance, Baroque and more, are still in use today, they just have little different names, for example Neoclassical architecture today is called New Classicism. These antique styles are often called "Revivals".

Art Nouveau (1890-1914)

Art Nouveau wasn't only in architecture but in glasswork, interior design, jewelry, furniture and visual arts. Its a popular style that was made deliberately, spread from Europe to also United States. It became famous due to it's artwork and luxury, often represented curve and scroll-shaped railings which some of them were really thin, flowers, leaves, vines, animals and other natural stuff were painted on furniture, doors, ceramics and rarely on walls. The decorations were everywhere, inside and outside, the outsides sometimes included bunch of different colors.

Beaux Arts (1895-1925)

Beaux-Arts architecture involved from French neoclassicism. It was important style in France which have used Baroque and Renaissance elements, basically modern-like materials, such as iron and glass. Its also known as Academic Classicism or Classical Revival which is descbribed by order, formal design, grandiosity, flat roof, arched windows, symmetry, subtle polychromy and elaborate details. Overall its a combination of classical Roman and Greek architecture with ideas that are Renaissance-like decorations.

Neo-Gothic (1905-1930)

Neo-Gothic is simply Gothic Revival where it shows further modernism. People have used Gothic Revival ideas on modern skyscrapers, thats why its called Neo-Gothic. These skyscrapers often have arched and pointed windows, strong vertical lines - they represent height, medieval carvings and little towers which are pinnacles. For example, one of these Neo-Gothic skyscrapers are Raymond Hood's 1922 Tribune Tower in Chicago.

Art Deco (1925-1937)

Art Deco firstly appeared in the 1910s which came from France. This style has strongly influenced buildings, ships, bridges, furniture and vehicles. It showed more technology, socialism and luxury, including materials such as ebony, ivory and also some thin craftsmanship. While the World War I was going, it became more unpretentious, so new materials came, containing stainless steel, chrome plating and plastic. Some years later it pretty much failed, some of the buildings were destroyed due to the World War II, so people stopped using this architecture style and the replacement was Modernist architecture.

Modernist Architecture (1900-Present)

Modernist architecture is known as Modern architecture which uses materials like a huge pile of glass, steel and ferroconcrete (reinforced concrete). The style rejected the details, decorations, classicism and embraced minimalism, including box-shaped design. This architecture emerged in the 1950s and replaced Art Deco architecture due to the WWII. It litteraly became popular because of the simplicity and the classicism removal - replaced with the complete opposite which is technology. There are dozens of styles in Modern Architecture, such as Deconstructivism, Formalism, Structuralism and Brutalism. Overall Modernist Architecture can have the most mysterious designes today which is also called Brutalism.

Postmodernism (1972-Present)

In the late 1990s, Postmodernism divided into several new 4 groups, containing neo-futurism, high-tech architecture, Deconstructivism and new classical architecture. Postmodernism have mimicked classical and historical-shaped styles on libraries, schools and other buildings, but they still often have unusual materials and the Minimalism look. For summary Postmodernism architecture is the classical revival and occassionally Brutalism, but no ornaments.

Neo-Modernism (1997-Present)

Neo-Modernism style often uses alot of present-day business-like architecture which is basically Modernism. Its all Modernism architecture which rejects the classical ornamentation, decorations and can even use a really basic design, a box-shaped house polished with white paint. Neo-Modernism began with the 1997 Guggenheim Museum success, and lastly some of this architecture still kind of copies the prehistorical and historical styles, for example the Marina Bay Sans Resort has pretty similar shape comparing to the Stonehenge.
